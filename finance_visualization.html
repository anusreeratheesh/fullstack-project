1. Corporate Overview Infosys Limited, established in 1981 with an initial capital of US$250, has evolved into a global leader in next-generation digital services and consulting. Headquartered in India, the company operates across 59 countries with 292 offices worldwide. As of fiscal year 2025, Infosys employs 3,23,578 professionals serving 1,869 active clients globally. The organization pioneered the Global Delivery Model and became the first Indian IT company listed on NASDAQ. With a market capitalization of approximately US$75.8 billion, Infosys continues to maintain a strong, debt-free balance sheet supported by high liquidity and disciplined capital allocation. The company’s purpose — “To amplify human potential and create the next opportunity for people, businesses and communities” — drives its enterprise AI-first transformation strategy, global expansion, innovation-led growth, and stakeholder value creation. 2. These financial indicators demonstrate operational efficiency, strong margin management, and disciplined growth. 3. Revenue Mix & Geographic Distribution Revenue distribution reflects diversified global exposure. In FY 2024-25, North America contributed 57.9% of total revenues, Europe accounted for 29.8%, Rest of the World contributed 9.2%, and India represented 3.1%. This geographic mix underscores Infosys’ strong presence in developed markets while maintaining expanding footprints in emerging economies. Sector diversification across Banking & Financial Services, Manufacturing, Retail, Energy, Utilities, Communications, Healthcare, and Life Sciences reduces concentration risk. Strategic client partnerships and long-term digital transformation engagements continue to drive recurring revenue stability and large-deal momentum across geographies. 4. Strategic Priorities & Enterprise AI Infosys’ strategy centers on Enterprise AI transformation. Through Infosys Topaz, the company integrates Artificial Intelligence, Cloud (Infosys Cobalt), Data architecture, Cybersecurity, Automation, and Digital platforms to enable AI-first enterprises. The organization is investing in generative AI, agentic AI systems, hybrid multi-cloud environments, automation frameworks, and domain-specific AI models. Focus areas include cost optimization, modernization of legacy systems, intelligent operations, predictive analytics, cybersecurity resilience, and data fabric architectures. Enterprise AI is positioned as a monetization engine, helping clients improve productivity, optimize supply chains, enhance customer experience, and accelerate innovation cycles. 5. ESG & Sustainability Performance Infosys continues to lead in Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) performance. The company has remained carbon neutral for six consecutive years across Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions. It has installed 60 MW of solar captive power plants and implemented carbon offset programs benefiting over 2,72,000 rural families. Water sustainability initiatives include 11 lake rejuvenation projects, adding 4.3 billion liters to water holding capacity.
